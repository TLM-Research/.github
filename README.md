# TLM Research

**An independent research initiative advancing temporal market design for blockchain protocols through protocol economics and mechanism design, with Ethereum as the initial research platform.

---

## Overview

TLM Research explores how **time** can become a first-class economic dimension in decentralized systems.

Modern blockchain protocols have significantly advanced blockspace allocation through innovations such as protocol-managed fee markets and proposer-builder separation. These mechanisms have greatly improved price discovery, market efficiency, and decentralized block construction.

Yet one important aspect of user demand remains largely implicit:

> **How should decentralized systems represent and coordinate users' temporal preferences?**

TLM (Temporal Liquidity Market) investigates this question by studying temporal market design, protocol economics, and distributed systems, with Ethereum serving as the initial research platform.

---

## Mission

Our mission is to advance the theory and practice of **temporal market design** for decentralized systems.

Rather than proposing immediate protocol changes, this project seeks to:

* understand existing protocol designs,
* identify open research questions,
* develop conceptual frameworks,
* explore candidate mechanisms,
* encourage discussion and collaboration.

---

## Current Research Areas

Current work focuses on:

* Ethereum fee markets (EIP-1559)
* Proposer-Builder Separation (PBS)
* Enshrined PBS (EIP-7732)
* MEV and builder optimization
* Temporal preference representation
* Protocol-visible information
* Temporal market design
* Distributed systems and mechanism design

---

## Research Philosophy

This repository is **not** intended to present a finished protocol proposal.

Instead, it serves as an open research initiative that:

* builds upon existing Ethereum research,
* surveys related work,
* documents evolving ideas,
* identifies trade-offs,
* records open questions.

Individual mechanisms—including Temporal Liquidity Reserve (TLR) and other future proposals—should be viewed as candidate implementations rather than final conclusions.

## Guiding Principle

Ethereum made **price** protocol-visible through protocol-managed fee markets.

TLM explores whether **temporal preference** can likewise become a protocol-visible economic signal while preserving decentralized market principles.
